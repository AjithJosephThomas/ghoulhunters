# STEM MAD — Requirements and Findings

**Program:** STEM MAD (Making A Difference)  
**Organiser:** Catholic Network Australia; Brisbane Catholic Education (BCE) participates  
**Project:** Ghoulhunters — Community Gold Clam Watch  
**Year level:** Year 5 (Primary)  
**Purpose:** Everything our team needs to meet STEM MAD expectations

---

## 1. What is STEM MAD?

STEM MAD invites Catholic school students to use **Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics** to solve **real-world problems** and act for the **common good**.

- Linked to **Laudato Si’** — caring for our common home (environment)  
- Schools compete in **local / diocesan** showcases first  
- Top projects may be nominated to **STEM MAD National** (often in Brisbane)  
- BCE schools participate through Brisbane Catholic Education

**Official links:**

- [STEM MAD — CNA](https://www.cna.catholic.edu.au/stem-mad)  
- [STEM MAD National](https://www.cna.catholic.edu.au/stem-mad-national)

## 3. Judging criteria (what scores points)

National submissions are evaluated on:

| Criterion | What judges want | How Ghoulhunters fits |
|---|---|---|
| **Design process** | Iterative cycle: idea → build → test → improve | Design journal with v1, v2, user test results |
| **Curriculum links** | STEM subjects connected | Science (species), Tech (app, QR), Engineering (poster, UX), Maths (GPS, map) |
| **Innovation** | Creative use of science and maths | Poster + QR + app as one system for local river |
| **Engineering & communication** | Clear, confident presentation | Live demo, simple slides, every student speaks |
| **Catholic distinction** | Making a Difference; care for environment | Protect Brisbane River; Laudato Si’ link |

### Primary vs secondary

- **Year 5 competes in PRIMARY** category — not against secondary robotics/coding projects  
- Judges expect age-appropriate depth, not professional software  
- **Working demo** matters more than complexity

## 4. Project criteria checklist (Primary)

Use this before showcase day:

### Design process
- [ ] Problem identified with real-world evidence  
- [ ] Research from trusted sources (Queensland Government)  
- [ ] Sketches or wireframes of first design  
- [ ] Built a prototype  
- [ ] Tested with users (classmates, parents)  
- [ ] Improved design after feedback (document what changed)  
- [ ] Design journal or blueprint shows the journey  

### Curriculum links
- [ ] **Science:** invasive species, ecosystem, “don’t move” spreads larvae  
- [ ] **Technology:** website, camera, GPS, QR code  
- [ ] **Engineering:** designing poster and user-friendly screens  
- [ ] **Mathematics:** coordinates, map, maybe count test results (8/10 understood)  

### Innovation
- [ ] Clear why our solution is useful for Brisbane  
- [ ] More than one deliverable (not just a poster)  

### Communication
- [ ] Every team member speaks  
- [ ] Live demo practised 10+ times  
- [ ] Backup if technology fails (video or screenshots)  

### Making a Difference
- [ ] Explains who benefits (community, river, wildlife)  
- [ ] Connects to caring for creation / common good  
- [ ] Optional: Council email, poster mock-up, user test numbers

## 7. Excellence tips for Year 5 (BCE / National)

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Live phone demo | Only talking about an idea |
| Design journal with before/after | One version only |
| User test with numbers (8/10) | “We think people will like it” |
| All students on video and at booth | One student does everything |
| Correct biosecurity facts | Wrong health claims or “eradication by students” |
| Printed poster at booth | Only digital poster |
| Practise demo with bad Wi‑Fi plan | First-time demo on the day |
