# Queensland Compliance — Requirements and Findings

**Project:** Ghoulhunters — Community Gold Clam Watch (student prototype)  
**Audience:** Year 5 team, teachers, parents  
**Purpose:** Follow the law and official biosecurity guidance when we build posters, website, and app  
**Important:** We are students learning — our prototype does **not** replace official reporting.

---

## 1. Why this document matters

Even a **school project** must use **correct facts and messages** about freshwater gold clam. Wrong advice (e.g. “pick it up” or false health claims) can:

- Mislead the public  
- Break biosecurity rules if someone follows bad advice  
- Hurt our STEM MAD score (research quality)

This document lists **Queensland rules and official guidance** in simple language.

## 2. The pest — official facts

| Fact | Official information |
|---|---|
| **Scientific name** | *Corbicula fluminea* |
| **Common name** | Freshwater gold clam (also Asian clam) |
| **First in Australia** | Detected Brisbane River area, 2023 |
| **Queensland status** | **Established invasive pest** — managed to reduce spread and impact |
| **Eradication** | **Not considered technically feasible** at national level; focus is surveillance and containment |
| **Known areas (examples)** | Colleges Crossing, Savages Crossing, Riverside Park, Somerset Dam — 70 km+ along Brisbane River |
| **Identification** | Small shell, often under 25 mm; yellow-brown to black; **evenly spaced ridges** on shell |

**Sources:**

- [Freshwater gold clam — Business Queensland](https://www.business.qld.gov.au/industries/farms-fishing-forestry/agriculture/biosecurity/animals/invasive/restricted/freshwater-gold-clam)  
- [DPI — Freshwater gold clam management in Queensland](https://dpi.engagementhub.com.au/freshwater-gold-clam)  
- National guidelines for management of *Corbicula fluminea* (V1.0)

## 3. What the law requires (simple)

### 3.1 Biosecurity Act 2014 (Queensland)

Freshwater gold clam is **restricted matter** (multiple categories). For our messages:

| Rule | What it means for us |
|---|---|
| **Do not move** the clam | Posters and app must say: do not pick up, move, keep, or take home |
| **Do not release** into environment | Do not put clams in other waterways |
| **Report category 2 restricted matter** | If someone is aware of it in certain situations, they must report to an **authorised officer** within **24 hours** |
| **General Biosecurity Obligation (GBO)** | Everyone must take reasonable steps not to spread pests |
| **Minimise risk (s.42(5))** | Do not do things that make spread worse |

**Official reporting (Biosecurity Queensland):**

- Phone: **13 25 23**  
- Online: [Report a biosecurity pest or disease](https://www.dpi.qld.gov.au/contact/report-a-biosecurity-pest-or-disease)  
- **Within 24 hours** of becoming aware (for required reporting situations)

### 3.2 What our student app must say

| Must include | Must NOT say |
|---|---|
| “Do not touch or **move** the clam” | “Pick it up to identify” |
| “Moving mud/water spreads the pest” | “Touching causes skin irritation” (not official QLD message) |
| “Report to Biosecurity Queensland: **13 25 23**” | “Our app is the only report you need” |
| “Student prototype — also use official reporting” | “We will eradicate all clams” |
| “Take a photo if safe” | “Collect samples for us” |

## 4. What Queensland does NOT ask students to do

| Activity in original idea | Compliance status |
|---|---|
| Volunteer **collection** of clams | **Not allowed** without authorisation — moving/keeping restricted matter |
| Student **eradication** program | **Not aligned** — official approach is management, not eradication |
| Hand clams to **fertilizer factory** | **Not** a public/volunteer activity — requires permits and biosecurity controls |
| **ML deletes** real sightings | **Risky** — false negatives could hide real pests |

**For STEM MAD:** Describe these as **“not part of our school prototype”** and explain we support **official reporting** only.

## 5. Correct public messages (use on poster, app, slides)

### 5.1 DO

- Take a **photo** from the bank if safe  
- **Note location** (GPS or landmark)  
- **Report** to Biosecurity Queensland within **24 hours**  
- **Check, clean, dry** boats, trailers, fishing gear (optional extra for posters)  
- Tell an adult  

### 5.2 DO NOT

- **Touch, pick up, or move** the clam  
- Take clam home  
- Move water, mud, or shells to another place  
- Use as bait or aquarium stock  

### 5.3 Why “don’t move” (kid-friendly science)

- Clams can spread **larvae** in water  
- **Mud** on shoes and boats carries eggs or young clams  
- Disturbing the riverbed can spread them to new spots

## 7. Privacy and data (simple)

Our prototype may collect **photos** and **GPS location**. That can be **personal information**.

| Requirement | What we do |
|---|---|
| Tell users what we collect | Short privacy notice on report page |
| School demo only | Say data used for STEM MAD demonstration |
| Consent | Checkbox before submit |
| Don’t share publicly | Don’t publish exact home addresses; use demo map with teacher permission |
| Adult help | Teacher/parent sets up form storage securely |

If a **Queensland government agency** ran a real app, stricter privacy laws (Information Privacy Act 2009) would apply. Our school project still should be **respectful and safe**.
