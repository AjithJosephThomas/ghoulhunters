# Ghoulhunters — Distillations

**Project:** Community Gold Clam Watch (student prototype)  
**Audience:** Year 5 STEM MAD team, teachers, parents  
**Purpose:** Short summary of everything we need to know to do this project well.

---

## What we are building

Ghoulhunters is a **school STEM MAD project** — not a real government app.

We help people who visit the **Brisbane River** to:

1. **Learn** what freshwater gold clam looks like  
2. **Know** not to touch or move it  
3. **Report** a sighting using our **website/app prototype** (photo + location)  
4. **Remember** to also report to **Biosecurity Queensland** (the official way)

---

## What we are NOT building (for this project)

| Not in our project | Why |
|---|---|
| Eradication program | Queensland manages gold clam with **surveillance**, not student eradication |
| Volunteers collecting clams | Against biosecurity laws — do not pick up or move clams |
| Machine learning that deletes reports | Too complex; not needed for Year 5 prototype |
| Rewards and lucky draws | Too complex for now |
| Replacing official reporting | Our app is a **learning tool**; BQ is the legal reporting channel |

---

## Key facts (research)

- **Species:** Freshwater gold clam (*Corbicula fluminea*)  
- **Where:** Brisbane River area (e.g. Colleges Crossing, Savages Crossing)  
- **Problem:** Spreads fast; harms river health and equipment  
- **What to do:** Take a photo, note location, **do not move it**  
- **Official report:** Biosecurity Queensland — **13 25 23** or online, within **24 hours**  
- **Sources:** Queensland Department of Primary Industries; Brisbane City Council biosecurity pages

**Good:** We explain the problem and show slides.  
**Excellent:** Live phone demo + poster + design journal + user testing (“9 out of 10 understood don’t move it”).

## Messages we always use

- **“See it. Snap it. Report it. Don’t move it.”**  
- **Do NOT touch or move** freshwater gold clam  
- **Moving spreads the pest** (mud, water, boats)  
- **Also report to Biosecurity Queensland: 13 25 23**
