Our mission

We help people learn about invasive species in our region, report sightings safely, and protect local ecosystems — without moving pests. Each species has its own watch program with a species guide and outreach plan.

How every program works

  1. Learn — read the species guide on this website or on our posters
  2. See — spot something that matches the identification guide
  3. Photo — take a picture from a safe distance (do not move it)
  4. Remember location — note where you are
  5. Report officially — call Biosecurity Queensland 13 25 23 within 24 hours

Program cycle

Each Ghoulhunters watch program runs as a continuous community cycle:

  • Educate — share official government facts about the species
  • Watch — encourage the community to look for suspected pests
  • Identify — help people recognise the species and native look-alikes
  • Report — guide people to official biosecurity reporting
  • Prevent — promote check, clean, dry for boats and gear where relevant
  • Outreach — posters, QR codes, and community awareness

Program tools

What we do not do

  • We do not collect or move invasive species
  • We do not replace official biosecurity reporting
  • We do not claim to eradicate pests — we support awareness and safe reporting
