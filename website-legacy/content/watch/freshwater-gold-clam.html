Important: Queensland manages freshwater gold clam through surveillance and containment, not community eradication. This watch project focuses on education, biodiversity protection, and helping people report to Biosecurity Queensland the right way.

Overview

The Nature Ninjas Gold Clam Watch is a community response to an invasive species in the Brisbane River. We teach the community how to recognise the clam and report it without harming the environment.

Project goals

  • Increase awareness of invasive gold clam
  • Help people identify the species from photos and facts
  • Place posters with QR codes near the river (with permission)
  • Provide a website for learning and reporting guidance
  • Always direct people to official reporting: 13 25 23

Community role

Everyone can be a citizen scientist:

  1. Learn what the clam looks like
  2. If you see one, do not move it
  3. Take a photo and note the location
  4. Report to Biosecurity Queensland within 24 hours

Future vision (Phase 2)

We hope to add:

  • A simple report form on this website
  • Partnership conversations with Brisbane City Council for awareness
  • More languages for river visitors

We will not ask volunteers to collect, kill, or transport clams — that is for authorised biosecurity officers only.
